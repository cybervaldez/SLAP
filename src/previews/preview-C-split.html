The Model

Three layers. One methodology. The SLAPstick framework dissects AI-generated content through escalating lenses of critique.

LAYER 1: THE PERFORMER

Proud-slop content — the AI's best effort, unedited

↓

LAYER 2: THE CRITICS

Expert panels + user personas roast every section

↓

LAYER 3: THE REDEMPTION

9 styled variations — same words, transformed design

Why This Works

The slop is the control group. The critics are the diagnosis. The variations are the proof that design — not better copy — is the cure.

Each variation keeps the identical text. Only the visual design system changes. Scores shift from 2-3 to 5-8.

// How We Almost Ruined It

The roast was so good people forgot we were demonstrating design skills.

The expert panels landed. The persona quotes were sharp. The comedy hit. But somewhere along the way the project started reading as standup comedy, not a design study.

We had to recalibrate — less comedy set, more design critique with personality. The humor stays, but it serves the argument now, not the other way around.

The hardest part was not writing the slop. It was resisting the urge to fix it before the critics had their say.
