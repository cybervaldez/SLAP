What happens when you let AI write your website... and then roast it

The Problem

Every AI page looks the same.

Purple gradient. Inter font. Rounded-full buttons. "Revolutionize your workflow." You've seen this page a thousand times. That's because AI wrote it a thousand times.

The Discovery

We built the worst page on purpose.

The content is intentionally bad AI writing — proud-slop, the kind that sounds confident while saying nothing. Then we fed it through three layers:

Layer 1 — The Performer AI-generated marketing copy. Unedited. Proud of itself.
Layer 2 — The Critics Five expert personas score every section. Marketing, UX, Design, Product, Technical.
Layer 3 — Redemption Can visual design rescue doomed content? Or does it just put lipstick on slop?
The Proof

Same words. Nine designs. Scores from 2 to 8.

Identical content. Wildly different reactions. Design is not decoration.

The Tension

Design can't save bad content.
But it can expose or elevate it.

The same empty paragraph reads completely differently depending on its frame:

Brutalist exposes — strips away polish, forces the reader to confront hollow words naked.
Warm Organic hides — wraps clichés in soft textures until they feel like wisdom.
Art Deco raises expectations — gilded framing makes mediocre copy feel like a broken promise.

This is the core finding. Design is a lens, not a bandage.

Author Notes

How we almost ruined it by being too funny.

Confession time.

Halfway through the study, the critics got too good at roasting. We were laughing so hard at Marketing Persona's takedowns that we started optimizing for comedy instead of insight.

The UX Persona would say something devastating like "This hero section has the information density of a fortune cookie" and we'd high-five instead of fixing the methodology.

We had to pull back. The comedy was a symptom of the same disease we were studying — prioritizing surface over substance. We kept the jokes. But we made sure the scores were honest.

The irony of almost producing slop about slop was not lost on us.
