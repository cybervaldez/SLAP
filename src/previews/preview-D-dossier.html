The Crime Scene

Conclusion of Investigation

Design cannot save bad content, but it can expose or elevate it.

Art Deco & Retro-Futurism scored highest — strong visual identity created enough friction to slow readers down, making them notice the content rather than skim past it.
Maximalist & Memphis scored lowest — visual noise amplified the emptiness of the copy. More decoration on hollow words made the hollowness louder.
Brutalist was the most honest frame — it stripped away every decoration, leaving the AI slop nowhere to hide. Experts found it "refreshingly transparent."
Neo-Minimal created a false sense of authority. Clean whitespace made generic copy feel more credible than it deserved. This is the most dangerous pattern.
SLAP baseline with intentional anti-slop callouts performed well: the meta-commentary forced readers to engage critically rather than passively.
